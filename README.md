# ARE WE IDIOCRACY YET?

> *"I like money."* — Frito Pendejo

**[idiocracy.wtf](https://idiocracy.wtf)** tracks how dangerously close our timeline is to Mike Judge's 2006 documentary... sorry, *comedy film* about a future where humanity has gotten so dumb that a perfectly average guy becomes the smartest person on the planet.

Turns out the movie was 20 years early, not 500.

## What Is This, Scrote?

The **Idiocracy Proximity Index** is a carefully calibrated, peer-reviewed*, scientifically rigorous** measurement of how close reality has drifted toward the world of President Dwayne Elizondo Mountain Dew Herbert Camacho.

Each comparison card puts a prediction from the film side-by-side with something that actually happened. In the real world. Where we all live. On purpose, apparently.

Categories include **Politics**, **Corporate**, **Entertainment**, **Education**, **Healthcare**, **Environment**, **Science**, **Justice**, and **Culture** — because the decline is *comprehensive*.

<sub>*Not peer-reviewed.</sub>
<sub>**Not scientifically rigorous. But neither is anything else anymore, so.</sub>

## Why Does This Exist?

Because in 2006, Mike Judge made a movie about a future where people water crops with sports drinks, the most popular show on TV is a guy getting hit in the nuts, and the President solves problems by firing a machine gun into the ceiling.

And then all of that started happening.

Someone had to keep score.
